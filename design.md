# Aroma — Design System

> Adapted from the original "AROMA Hotel Boutique — Valle de Guadalupe" brand book (Nov 6, 2023) for **Aroma Events**.
> **Important:** This adaptation drops the *hotel/property* framing entirely — Aroma Events is an event space, not a hotel. Nowhere in any asset, page, or image should "Hotel Boutique," or any wording implying a lodging property, appear. The logo lockup is **"AROMA"** only. **Valle de Guadalupe is kept** as the location context (the event space is physically there), used in copy and as a location line where appropriate — just never paired with "Hotel Boutique." All colors, typography, motifs, and sensory brand direction below carry over unchanged.

---

## 1. Brand Concept

**Big idea:** *Celebrating the Aroma of Life*

The brand is built on a single sensory metaphor: **aroma** — the sense most closely tied to memory, place, and people. Every touchpoint should evoke a feeling of gathering, connection, and indulgence, filtered through the ritual of wine and shared experience.

**Concept pillars** (use these as content/messaging anchors):
- The aroma of being together, of reconnecting, of celebrating a moment
- The aroma of a memory, a story, a conversation
- Aroma is synonymous with pleasant, even seductive, sensations — but is ephemeral, not lasting: "a new breath of life," "it's in our essence"

**Mission (adapted, no hotel/property reference):**
> We believe gatherings, paired with wine, provide the greatest opportunity to create connections and memories. Set in Valle de Guadalupe, Mexico's wine country, we want guests to live in the moment, indulge in one another, and celebrate the aroma of life.

**Location context:** Aroma Events is physically located in **Valle de Guadalupe, Baja California** — Mexico's premier wine region. The valley itself (vineyards, golden light, natural landscape, wine culture) is a core part of the brand's sensory story and should be referenced in copy, imagery, and location materials (maps, directions, "where we are" content). What's dropped is only the *hotel/lodging* framing — this is an event space in the valley, not a boutique hotel.

**Brand personality:** warm, sensory, elegant but unpretentious, feminine-leaning, present-focused, hedonist (in the healthy sense — pleasure, connection, indulgence, not excess).

## 2. Logo

- **Lockup:** a hand-painted, imperfect circular ring (like a wine stain / wine glass ring) in the primary wine-red, sitting above the wordmark **AROMA** set in the brand's thin geometric sans, all caps, wide letter-spacing.
- **Location line, no property line.** The original lockup carried two lines beneath the wordmark: "HOTEL BOUTIQUE" and "VALLE DE GUADALUPE." **Drop the first, keep the second.** The lockup may read:
  ```
        (ring mark)
         A R O M A
    VALLE DE GUADALUPE
  ```
  Same small-caps, wide-tracked treatment as the original secondary line — just the one line, never "Hotel Boutique."
- If a descriptor is ever needed in copy (not baked into the logo graphic), use "Aroma Events" in plain text set in body type — never inside the logo lockup itself. "Valle de Guadalupe" can be used freely in copy, addresses, maps, and location references.
- **Tagline** (optional, used in copy/footers, not necessarily in the lockup): *"Celebrating the aroma of life."*
- The ring is intentionally imperfect/hand-drawn (like a watercolor wine ring left on paper) — do not recreate it as a perfect vector circle. Texture and asymmetry are part of the mark's identity.
- A secondary, softer version of the mark exists: a solid, gradient-blurred circle (soft pink/terracotta glow, no ring outline) — usable as a background/watermark element, never as the primary logo.

## 3. Color Palette

Sampled directly from the brand book's color reference page. Cream is the primary background/canvas; wine is the primary accent; the rest are supporting/secondary tones.

| Role | Name | Hex | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Background (primary) | Cream | `#F3F1E5` | Default canvas for nearly all layouts |
| Brand accent (primary) | Wine / Maroon | `#651F27` | Logo ring, key CTAs, headline accents |
| Ink (primary) | Black | `#000000` | Used sparingly — high-contrast panels, not body text default |
| Secondary accent | Terracotta | `#C49279` | Warm supporting tone |
| Secondary accent (deeper) | Terracotta Dark | `#A5725A` | Wordmark-on-light variant, secondary text accents |
| Secondary accent | Olive / Gold | `#A5975A` | Supporting panels, natural/earthy notes |
| Neutral | Warm Grey | `#D2D2D2` | Dividers, muted panels |
| Tertiary accent | Dusty Blue | `#5880B2` | Used sparingly — diagrams, occasional contrast panel, not a core brand color |
| Soft glow accent | Blush Pink | gradient of `#E8B4AE` → transparent | Soft circular glow motif, watermarks |

**Usage guidance:**
- Cream is the default background everywhere; wine is the signature accent color (the "aroma"/wine-stain color) and should anchor the logo, key headlines, and primary buttons/links.
- Terracotta, olive, and grey are secondary/supporting — good for tags, secondary sections, alternating content blocks.
- Blue is an outlier accent from the original deck (used mostly in internal strategy diagrams) — treat as optional/rare, not a core brand color for guest-facing material.
- Avoid heavy black fields as a default; black works as an occasional high-contrast moment (e.g., a dark section), not the base palette.

## 4. Typography

- **Display / headline type:** a thin-weight, geometric sans-serif (Futura/Avenir/Century-Gothic family character) set in **ALL CAPS with wide letter-spacing**. This is used for every section title and the logotype itself. It should read as airy, minimal, understated — never bold or heavy.
- **Body copy:** a clean, simple sans-serif at normal tracking and sentence case — utilitarian, high legibility, no personality competing with the display face.
- **Hierarchy pattern:** wide-tracked thin caps for titles/labels/eyebrow text → normal-weight sentence-case sans for paragraph copy. Avoid mixing more than these two type treatments.
- **Tone of type layout:** generous whitespace, centered or left-aligned title blocks, plenty of breathing room — never dense or cluttered.

## 5. Imagery & Mood

Pull from these visual themes when sourcing or shooting photography/video (none of this requires a specific property — all are mood/lifestyle references):

**People & moments**
- Golden-hour sunflares, backlighting
- Relaxation, wellness (hammocks, stretching, laughing together)
- Hugs, smiles, laughter, togetherness
- Hands picking grapes, twirling in the sun, walking through a vineyard
- Candid, real — not overly posed

**Objects & textures close-ups**
- Pouring wine, wine glass swirling, finishing a bottle
- Wine stains / wine rings on paper or table (a literal callback to the logo mark)
- Cork, old wood, iron, textured plaster walls
- Grapes, vines, leaves, hanging grape clusters
- Local craft textures: woven textiles, pottery, handmade baskets (generic artisanal/natural texture, not tied to a specific region's branding)

**Architecture & place**
- Clean, minimal, warm architecture — white/adobe walls, indoor-outdoor spaces, natural materials
- Open spaces, cozy intimate details in the same frame
- Valle de Guadalupe's actual landscape: rolling vineyard rows, desert-valley hills, wide open sky — use real valley scenery (vineyards, hillsides, golden desert light) rather than generic wine-country stock

**Effects & treatment**
- Soft timelapse, slow drone/establishing shots
- Circular motifs, framing, or masks in photo/video treatment (echoing the ring logo)
- Warm, natural color grading — nothing oversaturated or cold

**Avoid:** stock-photo stiffness, cold/blue-toned imagery, cluttered compositions, anything that reads as generic corporate hospitality marketing.

## 6. Graphic Motifs

- **The ring/circle is the core recurring device** — used as:
  - The logo mark itself
  - Concentric circle line-art as a background/framing device on title or section-opener layouts
  - A soft blurred circle as a watermark or background accent
  - A literal "wine ring" stain graphic as a decorative element
- Favor circular crops for photography where it fits (portraits, detail shots) to reinforce the mark.

---

## 7. Voice & Tone

- Warm, sensory, present-tense — write like you're describing an experience happening now, not a list of amenities/features.
- Lead with feeling before fact: connection, memory, indulgence, celebration — then specifics.
- Evocative, simple language. Short, poetic phrases work well as headlines (e.g., "Uncork some together time," "A breath of fresh air," "Pour yourself a glass of getaway").
- Primary voice leans toward addressing women/gatherings-of-women as the core audience, but should read as inclusive and warm to couples, friend groups, and all guests — never exclusionary in tone.
- Avoid: corporate stiffness, superlative-stuffed hotel-marketing clichés, anything transactional-sounding.

---

## 8. Do's and Don'ts

**Do:**
- Keep the logo to "AROMA" + the ring mark + (optionally) the "VALLE DE GUADALUPE" location line only.
- Use "Valle de Guadalupe" freely in copy, location pages, maps, and directions — it's the real, correct location and reinforces the wine-country story.
- Use cream as the default canvas and wine as the signature accent.
- Use thin wide-tracked caps for titles, simple sans for body copy.
- Lean into sensory, emotional, present-moment language and imagery.
- Reinforce the circular/ring motif wherever it fits naturally.

**Don't:**
- Don't include "Hotel Boutique" (or any lodging/property framing) in any logo, image, copy, or asset — Aroma Events is an event space, not a hotel.
- Don't render the ring mark as a perfect, sterile vector circle — preserve its hand-painted imperfection.
- Don't default to black backgrounds or heavy dark palettes — cream is the home base.
- Don't use bold/heavy display type — the brand voice in type is deliberately light and airy.
- Don't let messaging turn generic-corporate or amenity-list-driven — keep it sensory and human.
