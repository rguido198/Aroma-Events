Event Planning Checklist

A general timeline for planning your event at Aroma Events — adjust it to fit the occasion.

Every event here is different, but the planning timeline tends to follow a similar shape, whether you're booking a wedding, a corporate retreat, or a milestone celebration. This checklist is a general guide, not a strict rulebook — a rehearsal dinner needs a fraction of the lead time a 200-guest wedding does. Use it as a starting point, and talk with our team early about your date; the valley's high season books up faster than people expect.

9–12 Months Out

  • Set a guest count range and rough budget
  • Reach out to Aroma Events to check date availability and choose a space — Vineyard Terrace, Garden Courtyard, or Barrel Room
  • Send save-the-dates if travel is involved — Valle de Guadalupe is a destination for most guests
  • Start researching caterers, wine service, and other vendors for the valley

6 Months Out

  • Confirm your vendor list: catering, wine or bar service, photography, music
  • Plan guest lodging — Ensenada and the surrounding valley have the closest options
  • Finalize your guest list and start invitations
  • Walk the space again in person if you haven't recently, and confirm the layout for your guest count

1–2 Months Out

  • Confirm your final headcount
  • Finalize the day-of timeline — ceremony or start time, dinner service, toasts
  • Arrange transportation for guests unfamiliar with the valley's roads, especially for evening events
  • Confirm any AV, seating, or setup needs directly with our team

1–2 Weeks Out

  • Reconfirm final numbers with caterers and vendors
  • Share the day-of timeline with your wedding party, team, or key guests
  • Check the weather and confirm a backup plan for outdoor spaces
  • Pack for the valley — evenings cool down quickly even after a warm day

Day Of

  • Arrive with enough buffer for setup and vendor load-in
  • Designate one point of contact for last-minute questions
  • Take a moment before guests arrive — you did the planning, now let the evening happen
